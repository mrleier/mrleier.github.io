Activities (Left Align)

Historic Yorktown and beach - Located on the York River and only about 20 minutes away from Colonial Williamsburg, this is an excellent place to take a scenic drive to via the colonial parkway. There are multiple quaint shops and restaurants in addition to the beach.

Jamestown beach - Another great beach location, Jamestown beach is located off of the James River is more secluded than the Yorktown beach and does not feature any shops or restaurants. This is a great spot to go for a walk or to lay out on the beach.

Jamestown Settlement - The settlement itself provides an interactive experience and a trip back in time to the earliest days of the American colonies. Aside from the visitor center and settlement itself, there is a scenic loop through the Jamestown marsh which you can drive, walk or bike around and provides excellent views of nature.

Colonial Williamsburg - There are a multitude of tours provided by the Colonial Williamsburg foundation which will provide you access into the various buildings including the Wythe House, the Governor's Mansion and all of the colonial shops (blacksmith, apothecary, etc.). However, you do not need to pay admission to merely stroll the beautiful grounds of Colonial Williamsburg.

William and Mary Campus - Just on the other side of confusion corner from Colonial Williamsburg, ancient campus and old campus is great place for a casual walk. Be sure to take a stroll through the sunken gardens and walk by the Crim Dell Bridge (one of the most beautiful spots on any college campus).
